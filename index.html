Clever Confuses

Clarity Converts

Most email marketing specialists don't give a second thought to accessibility. Many exchange clarity for cleverness—and neither they nor their clients realize how much this might be hurting them.

"Accessibility is just a checkbox"

Accessibility is usability. If someone can't read, understand, or interact with your email, you're losing them—regardless of ability. Accessible design is good design.

"Our audience doesn't need accessible emails"

Everyone benefits from clarity. Busy executives, tired eyes, mobile users, neurodivergent readers, ESL speakers, older customers—all gain from accessible, well-structured emails.

"Plain Language is boring"

Plain Language is powerful. It's strategic, persuasive, and respectful of your reader's time. It doesn't dumb things down—it lifts conversions up.
